What this can and can't do

Everything runs in your browser — free, private, GitHub‑Pages friendly. That's also where the limits come from.

✓ Done on‑device

  • Goal, audience and mission shape realistic scenarios and the coaching tone.
  • Plain‑language feedback on pace, energy, pitch variation and pauses (raw charts kept under Advanced).
  • Filler words, repeated words and weak‑vocabulary detection with stronger‑word suggestions.
  • A motivational score with sub‑scores, section‑by‑section notes, achievements and a saved progress trend.
  • Webcam recording with an estimated eye‑contact read, plus an on‑device Whisper transcript.

✗ Needs AI/back‑end, so not included (or only estimated)

  • Judging what you actually said — argument quality, persuasiveness, factual accuracy — needs a language model. Feedback here is about delivery, not content.
  • True intro/body/conclusion analysis. Sections are split by time thirds, not by understanding your structure.
  • Real audience emotion. The engagement gauge is an estimate from your own pace, pitch variation, pauses and eye contact — not a read of listeners.
  • Eye contact is approximate — a webcam estimate of whether you're facing the camera, not calibrated eye‑tracking.
  • Reflection answers are saved, not analysed.
  • Scenarios are template‑based. Rich and varied, but not freshly AI‑generated.
  • Slide / presentation mode isn't built yet — it's feasible on‑device (upload images or a PDF, click through while recording) and would be the natural next addition.
